MITIGATION PLAYBOOK: SUPPLIER FAILURE & MATERIAL SHORTAGE

Event Classification: Supplier Bankruptcy / Component Shortage / Production Halt

Historical Reference:
- Semiconductor Supply Shock (2021–2022): TSMC capacity constraints; 6–12 month delays
- Japan Manufacturing Earthquake (2011): Supplier disruptions rippled globally for 6+ months
- COVID Lockdowns in Vietnam (2021): Manufacturing hubs frozen; 2–3 month supply gaps

Risk Indicators:
- Supplier financial distress signals (credit downgrades, missed payments, capacity warnings)
- Component shortage alerts from industry indices
- Manufacturing PMI index drops
- Geopolitical sanctions affecting supplier regions

Impact Window: 7–60 days (long-tail risk)
Affected SKUs: CHIP_AP, CHIP_MEM (high-concentration suppliers)

=== 3-POINT MITIGATION STRATEGY ===

Action 1: DUAL-SOURCING ACTIVATION (Execute within 24 hours)
- Shift 40–50% of affected SKU volume to pre-qualified backup suppliers:
  * China suppliers → Taiwan or South Korea alternatives
  * Single supplier → Distribute across 2–3 regional hubs
- Trigger emergency purchase orders (higher unit costs, reduced lead times)
- Unit Cost Premium: +10–18% for accelerated orders
- Timeline: 7–14 days vs. 30+ days normal

Action 2: INVENTORY PREPOSITIONING (Execute within 48 hours)
- Calculate 8–12 week consumption forecast (vs. normal 2–4 weeks)
- Acquire forward inventory from alternate suppliers
- Stage inventory across multiple ports/warehouses (geographic decentralization)
- Cost Delta: ₹500K–₹2M in bulk purchasing + storage (fully recoupable post-recovery)

Action 3: DEMAND MANAGEMENT & CUSTOMER COMMUNICATION (Execute within 12 hours)
- Issue Force Majeure notices to key customers (transparent communication)
- Negotiate allocation ratios (e.g., "70% of normal order quantity for next 6 weeks")
- Prioritize high-revenue customers; defer non-critical orders to post-recovery
- Trigger Dynamic Pricing: +5–15% margin on constrained SKUs
- Cost Delta: ₹100–300K in customer support + communication infrastructure

=== FINANCIAL SUMMARY ===
Total Mitigation Cost: ₹600K–₹2.5M
Revenue Impact Mitigated: ₹20–50M (via dual-sourcing + inventory buffer)
Customer Retention: 85–95% (vs. 40–50% without playbook)

=== POST-RECOVERY MONITORING ===
- Gradually reduce backup supplier orders as primary supplier stabilizes
- Rebuild inventory buffers to normal 3-week cycle over 8–12 weeks
- Update supplier risk scores in database; trigger Tier-1 supplier diversification review
- Implement supplier health KPI dashboard for early warning

=== ESCALATION PROTOCOLS ===
If shortage persists beyond 30 days:
- Activate Emergency Manufacturing: Contract local assembly (if tech-feasible)
- Trigger Regulatory Coordination: Government support for critical component imports
- Consider market hedging: Lock in futures contracts for next 6–12 months
