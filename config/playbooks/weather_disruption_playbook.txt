MITIGATION PLAYBOOK: EXTREME WEATHER & PORT DISRUPTION

Event Classification: Monsoon/Cyclone Season Port Closure

Historical Reference:
- Chennai Floods (Dec 2015): Port closure for 3 days; inventory buffers at secondary ports enabled recovery
- Cyclone Biparjoy (June 2023): Mundra port partial shutdown; Diwali demand peak delayed

Risk Indicators:
- IMD alerts for cyclone/heavy rainfall within 200km of port
- Wind speed > 60 km/h at port coordinates (Open-Meteo API)
- Port authority announcements of cargo operations suspension

Impact Window: 2–7 days (depends on cyclone intensity)
Affected Assets: Container handling equipment, crane operations, vessel scheduling

=== 3-POINT MITIGATION STRATEGY ===

Action 1: IMMEDIATE ROUTE DIVERSION (Execute within 2 hours)
- Redirect in-transit vessels from affected port to nearest alternate:
  * From JNPT (Mumbai) → Mundra or Paradip
  * From Chennai → Cochin or Vizag
  * From Mundra → JNPT
- Cost Impact: +₹15–25 per TEU (fuel + time premium)
- Inventory Impact: +2–3 days transit delay, but avoids port queue congestion

Action 2: SAFETY STOCK DEPLOYMENT (Execute within 6 hours)
- Activate emergency inventory at inland warehouses (Delhi, Bangalore regional DCs)
- Pre-position CHIP_AP, CHIP_MEM, PCB stocks from neighboring port-adjacent warehouses
- Minimum buffer: 3-week supply coverage at affected port's baseline demand
- Cost Delta: ₹50–100K in expedited cross-warehouse transport

Action 3: PROCUREMENT ACCELERATION (Execute within 12 hours)
- Contact alternate suppliers (Taiwan, South Korea backup sources) for expedited production
- Negotiate shorter lead times: 14 days → 7 days (premium: +8–12% on unit cost)
- Lock in air freight capacity for critical SKUs (CHIP_AP, memory modules)
- Cost Delta: +₹200–350K for accelerated procurement window; recoverable post-disruption

=== FINANCIAL SUMMARY ===
Total Mitigation Cost: ₹300–500K
Inventory Risk Avoided (Stockout Prevention): ₹5–10M (revenue protection)
ROI: 10–30x (1–2 week recovery window)

=== POST-RECOVERY MONITORING ===
- Resume normal port operations post-weather clearance (24–48 hour buffer)
- Normalize alternate inventory to pre-disruption levels over 10–14 days
- Update baseline forecasts post-event to reflect true demand spike during recovery period
