MITIGATION PLAYBOOK: PORT CLOSURE & INFRASTRUCTURE FAILURE

Event Classification: Port Authority Lockdown / Operational Shutdown

Historical Reference:
- Suez Canal Blockage (March 2021): Ever Given incident; 10-day global delay
- COVID Port Restrictions (2020): Port capacity reduced 40–60%; extended queues
- Labor Strikes (common in Indian ports): 3–7 day operational freezes

Risk Indicators:
- Port authority regulatory notices
- Labor union announcements of strikes/walkouts
- Geopolitical tensions affecting shipping lanes (Red Sea corridor)
- Infrastructure maintenance alerts

Impact Window: 3–14 days (depends on closure type)
Affected SKUs: All commodities (full port halt)

=== 3-POINT MITIGATION STRATEGY ===

Action 1: IMMEDIATE MODAL SHIFT (Execute within 4 hours)
- Switch from sea freight to air freight for high-value SKUs (CHIP_AP, memory modules)
- Redirect cargo to alternate port + overland trucking:
  * JNPT closure → Route via Mundra (350km, 2 days truck transit)
  * Chennai closure → Route via Cochin (500km, 3 days truck transit)
- Cost Impact: Air freight premium ₹80–150 per kg; overland ₹20–40 per unit
- Timeline Extension: +3–5 days total (vs. normal 7–10 day sea route)

Action 2: WAREHOUSE BUFFER ACTIVATION (Execute within 2 hours)
- Tap regional free-trade zone warehouses near closed port
- Release 2-week advanced inventory buffer to retail/manufacturing partners
- Activate emergency procurement from local assembly hubs (if applicable)
- Cost Delta: ₹100–200K in emergency storage/staging fees

Action 3: SUPPLIER COORDINATION (Execute within 6 hours)
- Negotiate temporary production pause with downstream manufacturers (no excess inventory buildup)
- Activate Demand Leveling Protocol: Smooth forecasted orders over extended window
- Communicate revised delivery timeline to key customers (transparent expectations)
- Cost Delta: ₹50–150K in customer communication + compliance documentation

=== FINANCIAL SUMMARY ===
Total Mitigation Cost: ₹300–700K
Lost Sales Risk Prevented: ₹8–15M
Operational Resilience Gain: 85–95% demand fulfillment (vs. 30–40% without playbook)

=== POST-RECOVERY MONITORING ===
- Resume normal port operations once authority clearance is issued
- Gradually normalize air/modal shift allocations back to sea baseline (5–7 days)
- Audit supplier inventory levels post-disruption; rebalance stock

=== CONTINGENCY TRIGGERS ===
If port closure extends beyond 7 days:
- Escalate to emergency manufacturing acceleration (Action 3b)
- Activate alternate source procurement (backup suppliers from alternative regions)
