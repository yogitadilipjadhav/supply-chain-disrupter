REGULATION PROFILE: BIS ADVANCED COMPUTING AND SEMICONDUCTOR MANUFACTURING EXPORT CONTROLS (OCTOBER 2022)
EFFECTIVE DATE: October 7, 2022 (some provisions phased) | ISSUER: US Bureau of Industry and Security (BIS), Department of Commerce
CITATION: 87 FR 62186 | EAR Control List additions: Multiple ECCNs including 3A090, 3B090, 3D001, 3E001 (new)
EXPORT_CONTROL_LEVEL: CRITICAL | GEOGRAPHY: China, Macau (primary destinations controlled)

The October 7, 2022 BIS rule was the most comprehensive US semiconductor export control action since the Cold War-era COCOM controls. It established a multi-layered framework targeting China's ability to develop and manufacture advanced semiconductors for AI, supercomputing, and military end-use applications, covering both finished chips and the equipment and materials needed to make them.

ADVANCED COMPUTING RULE — CHIP THRESHOLDS: The rule established performance parameters defining "advanced" chips subject to new export license requirements for China. The key threshold criteria: chips with both a processing performance above 4,800 TOPS (tera operations per second) at INT8 precision AND a performance density above 0.4 TOPS/mm². Alternative triggers: chips with bidirectional data transfer rate of 600 GB/s or more OR total processing performance above 4,800 TOPS. These thresholds were designed to capture NVIDIA A100, H100, AMD MI250X, and similar chips used in AI training clusters. Memory chips at or above HBM2 specification for bandwidth-intensive AI applications were also covered. Chips at or below these thresholds (including standard consumer GPUs, CPUs, mobile chips) were not affected.

SEMICONDUCTOR MANUFACTURING EQUIPMENT (SME) RULE: The rule added licensing requirements for equipment capable of producing chips at or below 16nm (or 14nm FinFET equivalent), including: atomic layer deposition (ALD) tools for gate dielectric and spacer applications below 5nm; deep UV lithography (DUV 193nm immersion) tools capable of multi-patterning for sub-28nm; advanced etch tools for fin patterning; ion implantation for source/drain engineering at advanced nodes. Extreme ultraviolet (EUV) tools were already de facto controlled (Netherlands ASML had informally restricted China sales since 2019). The SME controls were specifically designed to prevent Chinese foundries (SMIC, CXMT) from acquiring equipment needed to advance beyond their current technology node capabilities.

FOREIGN DIRECT PRODUCT RULE (FDPR) — CHINA ADVANCED COMPUTING: A new FDPR was established making non-US items that are the direct product of certain US software or technology subject to EAR controls when destined for China for covered advanced computing end-uses. This extended US jurisdiction beyond goods manufactured in the US to goods manufactured abroad using US equipment or technology, dramatically expanding the rule's reach.

ENTITY LIST ADDITIONS (October 7, 2022): 31 entities were added to the Entity List, including YMTC (Yangtze Memory Technologies Company — China's largest NAND flash manufacturer), CXMT (ChangXin Memory Technologies — leading Chinese DRAM manufacturer), Naura Technology Group (semiconductor equipment), Cambricon Technologies (AI chip designer), and other Chinese semiconductor and AI firms. Entity List designation requires US persons to obtain a BIS license before providing items on the Commerce Control List to listed entities, with a presumption of denial for most items.

US PERSONS RULE — SEMICONDUCTOR TALENT RESTRICTION: A provision addressed directly at Chinese semiconductor manufacturers' ability to employ US citizens, permanent residents, or companies: US persons are prohibited from supporting the development or production of advanced ICs at certain Chinese fab facilities without a BIS license. This provision was designed to prevent US semiconductor engineers and executives employed at Chinese chipmakers from advancing China's chip manufacturing capabilities.

SUPPLY CHAIN IMPLICATIONS FOR RISK CLASSIFIER: When `Export_Control_Level` is elevated (indicating a high score on this variable in the semiconductor_signals table for years 2022+), this rule is the primary regulatory driver. Orders touching Chinese-origin chips at or near the performance thresholds defined above, or involving equipment capable of advanced node manufacturing, warrant automatic CRITICAL or HIGH risk scoring regardless of other signal inputs. Chinese companies on the Entity List should be flagged for all transactions, not just those involving explicitly controlled items.
