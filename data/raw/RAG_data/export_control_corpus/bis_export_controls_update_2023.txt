REGULATION PROFILE: BIS EXPORT CONTROLS UPDATE — ADVANCED COMPUTING RULE EXPANSION (OCTOBER 2023)
EFFECTIVE DATE: October 17, 2023 (interim final rule) | ISSUER: BIS, US Department of Commerce
CITATION: 88 FR 73424 (Advanced Computing); 88 FR 73458 (Semiconductor Manufacturing Equipment)
EXPORT_CONTROL_LEVEL: CRITICAL | GEOGRAPHY: China, Macau, 21 additional "arms-embargoed" countries

The October 2023 BIS interim final rules represented the first major expansion of the October 2022 controls, closing gaps that had been identified in the first year of implementation and extending the framework to capture new chip architectures and additional manufacturing equipment categories.

ADVANCED COMPUTING RULE — EXPANDED THRESHOLDS: The 2023 rule introduced a new "performance density" threshold formula to capture chips that might fall below the 2022 absolute performance thresholds but represent advanced technology when normalized by die area. New threshold: chips with total processing performance above 4,800 TOPS AND performance density above 1.6 TOPS/mm² at INT8 precision. Additionally, the rule added new controls targeting gate-all-around (GAA) transistor architecture chips at or below 3nm, reflecting the transition from FinFET to GAA architecture by TSMC (N2 process), Intel (Intel 20A/18A), and Samsung (SF3). Chips using GAA at 3nm and below are subject to export license requirements regardless of their aggregate TOPS performance, as GAA represents the next frontier of semiconductor miniaturization.

LOOPHOLE CLOSURES — "KNOWLEDGE STANDARD" TIGHTENING: The 2023 rule tightened the "red flag" knowledge standard that had been used by some companies to argue they were unaware their products were being routed to controlled end-uses in China. The updated rule established clearer constructive knowledge standards: if information available to an exporter indicates that items are likely to be used for advanced computing or supercomputing end-uses in China, a license is required regardless of the stated destination or end-use at time of sale. This targeted intermediary brokers routing controlled chips through third countries.

ENTITY LIST EXPANSION — 40+ NEW ENTITIES: The October 2023 package added over 40 entities to the Entity List, including Chinese AI chip designers (Biren Technology, Moore Threads), Chinese wafer equipment companies, and Chinese cloud computing infrastructure providers that were using controlled AI chips. Huawei affiliates in the semiconductor domain received additional restrictions.

Not-Eligible-for-License-Exception (NELE) DESIGNATIONS: The 2023 rule designated certain advanced chips as "not eligible for license exception" — meaning normal BIS license exceptions (like ENC for encryption products or STA for exports to allies) cannot be used to export these items to China. Only a specific, individually reviewed license is acceptable, with a presumption of denial.

SEMICONDUCTOR MANUFACTURING EQUIPMENT EXPANSION: The SME rule was expanded to cover additional categories: (1) Gate-all-around (GAA) transistor fabrication equipment; (2) Advanced packaging equipment for 3D-IC integration below certain dimensional parameters; (3) Certain process control and metrology equipment with measurement capabilities below 5nm; (4) High-NA EUV lithography equipment (ASML's next-generation system enabling sub-2nm patterning) — preemptively controlled before system availability.

COORDINATED ALLY ACTION: The October 2023 rules were coordinated with Japan (which had implemented its own semiconductor equipment export controls in July 2023) and the Netherlands (which restricted ASML EUV and certain DUV tool exports to China). This "Trilateral" framework represents the first coordinated allied semiconductor export control regime, analogous in scope to Cold War-era COCOM.

RISK CLASSIFICATION IMPLICATION: Transactions involving any items produced by or for Chinese entities on the Entity List, any chips meeting the advanced computing thresholds, or any semiconductor manufacturing equipment capable of advanced node production should be automatically scored CRITICAL under this regulation framework for orders dated October 2023 onward.
