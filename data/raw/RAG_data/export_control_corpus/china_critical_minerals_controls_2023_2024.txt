REGULATION PROFILE: CHINA CRITICAL MINERAL EXPORT CONTROLS — ESCALATING SERIES (2023–2024)
EFFECTIVE DATES: Gallium/Germanium August 1, 2023; Graphite October 1, 2023; Antimony August 2024
ISSUER: China Ministry of Commerce (MOFCOM), Ministry of Industry and Information Technology (MIIT)
EXPORT_CONTROL_LEVEL: HIGH | GEOGRAPHY: China (exporter); US, Japan, EU, South Korea (targeted buyers)

China's critical mineral export control campaign began with gallium and germanium in 2023 and escalated through graphite and antimony in subsequent months, representing China's most significant use of raw material supply leverage in the context of the US-led semiconductor export control coalition. The controls directly impact semiconductor manufacturing, battery production, and electronics manufacturing supply chains.

GALLIUM (MOFCOM ORDER 13/2023 — EFFECTIVE AUGUST 1, 2023): China produces approximately 80 percent of global primary gallium as a byproduct of aluminum smelting using Chinese bauxite. Gallium metal is refined to 6N or 7N purity for semiconductor applications. The export licensing requirement applies to gallium metal, gallium compounds (gallium arsenide, gallium nitride, gallium phosphide), and certain gallium alloys. Exporters must apply for individual licenses; no automatic approvals are issued. The Ministry of Commerce reviews applications with no published criteria or timeline for approval decisions. Gallium's key semiconductor applications: (1) GaAs wafers — substrate for RF chips (5G front-end modules, satellite communications, defense radar); (2) GaN-on-Si or GaN-on-SiC — power electronics substrate for EV inverters, fast chargers, 5G base stations; (3) GaN-on-Si LED structures — LED backlights, general lighting. Global gallium demand is approximately 400-500 metric tons per year; China was producing 300+ metric tons annually prior to controls. Non-Chinese sources (Germany, Ukraine, South Korea, Japan) collectively produce less than 100 metric tons per year at current capacity.

GERMANIUM (MOFCOM ORDER 14/2023 — EFFECTIVE AUGUST 1, 2023): China controls approximately 60 percent of global germanium production, primarily as a byproduct of zinc smelting. Germanium's semiconductor applications: (1) SiGe heterojunction bipolar transistors (HBT) — used in 77GHz automotive radar, 5G mmWave RF chips, and optical communications; (2) High-efficiency multi-junction solar cells for space applications; (3) Germanium-based transistors for radiation-hardened electronics; (4) Fiber optic preforms — germanium dioxide (GeO2) is a key dopant for graded-index single-mode fiber. Non-Chinese germanium sources include Canada, Belgium (Umicore — secondary refining), and Russia. Germanium spot price rose approximately 50 percent following the July 2023 announcement, stabilizing at elevated levels.

GRAPHITE (MOFCOM — EFFECTIVE OCTOBER 1, 2023): China controls approximately 65 percent of natural graphite production globally and over 90 percent of synthetic graphite anode material production for lithium-ion batteries. While the primary impact is on EV battery supply chains, graphite's semiconductor relevance includes: (1) Graphite electrodes for semiconductor furnaces (thermal oxidation, diffusion, epitaxial growth); (2) EDM (electrical discharge machining) graphite electrodes used in tool and die manufacturing for semiconductor packaging molds; (3) High-purity graphite components in crystal growth furnaces for silicon wafer manufacturing. Battery supply chain disruption: battery-grade graphite controls create secondary effects on electronics product cost and supply through the shared upstream supply chain for lithium-ion cells used in smartphones, laptops, and tablets.

ANTIMONY (MOFCOM — EFFECTIVE AUGUST 2024): China produces approximately 55-60 percent of global antimony. Semiconductor and electronics relevance: (1) Antimony trioxide (Sb2O3) is a flame retardant synergist widely used in printed circuit board (PCB) FR4 epoxy resins and in connector housings — virtually every electronics PCB contains antimony-based flame retardants; (2) Antimony-doped semiconductor materials for certain high-speed transistors; (3) Lead-acid batteries used in UPS systems for data centers. The controls could affect PCB manufacturing supply chains for consumer electronics.

MITIGATION STRATEGY GUIDANCE: For each controlled mineral, mitigation actions follow a priority sequence: (1) Inventory accumulation while export licenses can still be obtained; (2) Qualification of non-Chinese sources (higher cost, lower volume); (3) Material efficiency engineering to reduce consumption per device; (4) Substitution where technically feasible (e.g., SiC replacing GaN for some power applications, silicon-based detectors replacing Ge-based for some IR applications); (5) Government engagement for strategic reserve programs. For the Mitigation Recommendation Agent: when Export_Control_Level is HIGH or CRITICAL for China-origin supply chains, recommend sourcing audit for gallium, germanium, graphite, and antimony content in the bill of materials.
