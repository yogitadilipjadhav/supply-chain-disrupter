POLICY PROFILE: INDIA SEMICONDUCTOR MISSION (ISM) — FRAMEWORK AND INCENTIVE SCHEMES (2021–PRESENT)
APPROVED: December 15, 2021 (Union Cabinet) | NODAL MINISTRY: Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY)
TOTAL OUTLAY: ₹76,000 crore (~$9.5–10 billion at 2021 exchange rates)
ADMINISTERED BY: India Semiconductor Mission (ISM), a specialized and independent nodal agency under MeitY

The India Semiconductor Mission represents India's comprehensive national strategy to develop domestic semiconductor manufacturing capability, reduce import dependency, and position India as a global hub for semiconductor production and design. The program was approved following the 2020-2021 global chip shortage, which exposed India's near-total dependence on semiconductor imports and accelerated policy action on electronic components self-sufficiency.

ISM SCHEME 1 — SEMICONDUCTOR FAB (SILICON WAFER FRONT-END MANUFACTURING): Incentive of 50 percent of project cost for establishing semiconductor wafer fabrication facilities in India. Eligible technology nodes: initially up to 28nm, with scope to support leading-edge nodes as ecosystem develops. The scheme targets both pure foundry (contract manufacturing) and integrated device manufacturer (IDM) business models. Companies must meet minimum investment thresholds (typically $1 billion+ for fab-scale projects) and maintain production in India for a minimum period. The Tata Electronics-PSMC Dholera project is the first approved project under this scheme.

ISM SCHEME 2 — DISPLAY FAB (TFT LCD AND AMOLED): Incentive of 50 percent of project cost for display panel manufacturing. Relevant to electronics supply chains: display panels (smartphone screens, TV panels, monitor panels) are a major component cost for consumer electronics and represent a significant portion of consumer electronics import value. India's ability to domestically manufacture display panels would reduce the electronics supply chain dependency on China (BOE, CSOT) and South Korea (Samsung Display, LG Display).

ISM SCHEME 3 — COMPOUND SEMICONDUCTOR / SILICON PHOTONICS / SENSOR FAB AND SEMICONDUCTOR ATMP/OSAT: For compound semiconductor manufacturing (GaAs, GaN, InP, SiC wafer fabs for power electronics, RF, photonics) and for ATMP (Assembly, Test, Marking, and Packaging) facilities: incentive of 50 percent of capital expenditure. This scheme specifically targets back-end semiconductor manufacturing, which has a lower capex threshold than front-end wafer fabs and can be operational more quickly (12-24 months vs. 36-48 months for front-end). Micron Technology India and CG Power+Renesas projects are approved under this scheme.

ISM SCHEME 4 — DESIGN-LINKED INCENTIVE (DLI): Incentive structure for Indian chip design companies (fabless design firms and India-based design centers of multinational companies) to develop products targeting the Indian and global markets. This includes a deployment-linked financial incentive and access to design infrastructure (EDA tools, prototype runs). The scheme aims to build India's indigenous design capability, which is currently concentrated in a few global semiconductor companies' India R&D centers (Intel, Qualcomm, Texas Instruments, Samsung, NXP — all with large India engineering centers in Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Pune, and Chennai).

STRATEGIC RATIONALE FOR FLIPKART-CONTEXT MITIGATION RECOMMENDATIONS: India's ISM program creates a multi-year trajectory toward domestic semiconductor manufacturing that Flipkart and other Indian e-commerce and electronics retailers can incorporate into long-term supply chain planning. Near-term (2024-2027): Micron Sanand OSAT provides India-based DRAM and NAND packaging capacity, reducing dependence on Taiwan and South Korean back-end packaging for memory chips used in smartphones, laptops, and storage products sold on Flipkart. Medium-term (2027-2030): Tata Electronics Dholera fab (28nm) would provide India-manufactured MCUs, display drivers, and power management ICs for domestic electronics brands and contract manufacturers. Long-term (2030+): India's DLI scheme aims to build domestic chip design capability, potentially enabling Made-in-India chip products for IoT, EV, and industrial applications.

PROCUREMENT IMPLICATIONS: For electronics supply chain risk mitigation involving Indian alternate sourcing, the Mitigation Recommendation Agent should cite ISM-approved facilities as preferred alternate sourcing routes when China-origin or Taiwan-origin supply chains face elevated risk scores. India-sourced alternatives carry lower Export_Control_Level risk (no BIS/Japan/Netherlands controls apply to India-destined exports), lower geopolitical risk, and preferential treatment under India's customs duty structure (PLI beneficiaries receive import duty waivers on manufacturing inputs).
