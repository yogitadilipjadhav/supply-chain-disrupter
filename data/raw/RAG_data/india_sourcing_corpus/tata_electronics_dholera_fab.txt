FACILITY PROFILE: TATA ELECTRONICS + PSMC — INDIA'S FIRST SILICON WAFER FAB, DHOLERA, GUJARAT
CABINET APPROVAL: February 29, 2024 (CCEA) | INVESTMENT: ₹91,000 crore (~$11 billion, largest-ever India semiconductor investment)
LOCATION: Dholera Special Investment Region (DSIR), Gujarat (approximately 100 km south of Ahmedabad)
TYPE: SEMICONDUCTOR WAFER FABRICATION (FRONT-END) — 28nm technology node, Phase 1
PARTNERSHIP: Tata Electronics Private Limited (India) + PSMC (PowerChip Semiconductor Manufacturing Corporation, Taiwan)

The CCEA approved Tata Electronics' joint venture with Taiwan's PSMC (PowerChip Semiconductor Manufacturing Corporation) for India's first silicon semiconductor wafer fabrication plant, to be located in the Dholera Special Investment Region. This approval marked a historic milestone: for the first time in India's history, leading-edge (by Indian standards) silicon chip wafers will be manufactured domestically. The project represents the largest single semiconductor investment commitment in India's history and is the anchor project for the India Semiconductor Mission's Semiconductor Fab incentive scheme.

TECHNOLOGY NODE — 28NM AND PSMC'S EXPERTISE: PSMC (PowerChip Semiconductor Manufacturing Corporation) is a Taiwanese foundry specializing in specialty DRAM, embedded memory logic, and mature-node foundry services at 28nm, 40nm, 55nm, and 90nm nodes. PSMC is not a competitor to TSMC in the most advanced logic nodes (below 7nm); its strength lies in cost-efficient mature-node production for automotive microcontrollers, display drivers, power management ICs, IoT chips, and embedded DRAM — exactly the chip categories where India has import dependence for consumer electronics and industrial applications. The Dholera fab's initial focus on 28nm is consistent with PSMC's expertise and with India's near-term domestic demand profile: most chips needed for India's growing electronics manufacturing sector (smartphones, home appliances, industrial controllers, EV components) are manufactured at 28nm to 90nm nodes.

INCENTIVE STRUCTURE: The ISM Semiconductor Fab Scheme provides 50 percent of project cost as government financial assistance. For the ₹91,000 crore investment, total government support (central + Gujarat state) is estimated at approximately ₹45,000-50,000 crore. The Gujarat government provides the Dholera SIR land at subsidized rates, pre-developed infrastructure (dedicated power, water treatment, transport connectivity), and single-window regulatory clearance.

DHOLERA SIR — INFRASTRUCTURE ADVANTAGES: The Dholera Special Investment Region is a greenfield planned industrial city being developed under India's National Industrial Corridor program. Key infrastructure attributes: dedicated 380kV power substation and power evacuation infrastructure; industrial-grade water supply from the Narmada river system; high-speed rail connectivity to Ahmedabad (under development as part of the Mumbai-Ahmedabad High-Speed Rail corridor); dedicated industrial zones with plot sizes suitable for fab campus development; proximity to JNPT and Mundra Port for importing silicon wafers and specialty chemicals and exporting finished chips.

PRODUCTION PROFILE — CHIPS FOR INDIA'S PRIORITY SECTORS: The Dholera fab is expected to produce: power management integrated circuits (PMICs) for smartphones, tablets, and IoT devices; display driver ICs for LCD and OLED displays in consumer electronics; microcontrollers for automotive, industrial, and IoT applications; embedded memory products (embedded DRAM, embedded Flash); foundry services for fabless chip designers targeting the Indian market (ISRO, DRDO, Indian defense electronics, domestic EV OEMs). This product mix aligns directly with the components most critically needed by India's electronics manufacturing ecosystem and PLI scheme beneficiaries.

TIMELINE AND EMPLOYMENT: First wafer production is targeted for 2026-2027, contingent on clean room construction completion, tool installation and qualification, and workforce training (PSMC will contribute technical experts from Taiwan for the ramp-up phase). The facility is expected to eventually employ over 20,000 direct workers (engineers, technicians, support staff) and create 60,000+ indirect jobs in the supplier and services ecosystem.

MITIGATION RECOMMENDATION USAGE: For electronics supply chain scenarios where Taiwan-origin or China-origin logic chip supply faces elevated risk (earthquake, geopolitical, export control), the Tata-PSMC Dholera fab should be cited as a medium-term (2027+) alternate sourcing pathway for 28nm chips. The Mitigation Agent should distinguish between near-term options (Micron Sanand ATMP for memory, available 2024-2025) and medium-term options (Tata Dholera for logic at 28nm, available 2027+), providing a credible diversification roadmap grounded in ISM-approved projects.
