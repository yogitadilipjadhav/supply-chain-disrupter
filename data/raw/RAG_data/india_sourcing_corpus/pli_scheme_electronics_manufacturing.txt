POLICY PROFILE: PLI SCHEME FOR LARGE-SCALE ELECTRONICS MANUFACTURING — INDIA'S ELECTRONICS SUPPLY CHAIN BACKBONE
APPROVED: April 1, 2020 (and subsequent phases) | ADMINISTERING MINISTRY: MeitY (Ministry of Electronics and IT)
TOTAL OUTLAY: ₹40,951 crore (~$5 billion) over 5 years (FY2021-FY2026)
BENEFICIARY CATEGORIES: Mobile phones, electronic components, IT hardware manufacturers operating in India

The Production Linked Incentive (PLI) scheme for Large-Scale Electronics Manufacturing represents India's primary policy instrument for building domestic electronics manufacturing capacity before the India Semiconductor Mission's semiconductor fab investments come online. While ISM targets upstream semiconductor manufacturing, the PLI scheme has already transformed India's downstream electronics assembly ecosystem, creating the customer base that will consume India-manufactured chips from Micron Sanand and the Tata Dholera fab.

PLI STRUCTURE — MOBILE PHONES AND ELECTRONIC COMPONENTS: The PLI scheme provides incentives of 4-6 percent of incremental sales over a base year for approved manufacturers establishing or expanding production in India. The mobile phone category attracted the largest investment, with Apple's contract manufacturers (Foxconn Technology Group through its India subsidiary, Wistron Corporation, and Pegatron Corporation) all receiving PLI approval to establish iPhone assembly operations in India. Indian manufacturer Dixon Technologies and Lava International expanded domestic production under PLI. Samsung Electronics India expanded its Noida factory under PLI for Android smartphone production.

INDIA'S ELECTRONICS MANUFACTURING TRANSFORMATION (2020-2024): The PLI scheme has produced measurable supply chain transformation. India's share of global mobile phone manufacturing increased from approximately 1 percent in 2014-15 (under the Make in India program's initial phase) to approximately 12-14 percent by 2023-24. India's mobile phone exports surged from $300 million in FY2018 to approximately $15+ billion in FY2024, with Apple iPhones accounting for a significant and growing share. Apple's stated target is to manufacture 25 percent of global iPhones in India by 2025-2026, with CEO Tim Cook citing India as a second home after China for Apple's manufacturing footprint.

ELECTRONICS SUPPLY CHAIN IMPLICATIONS: The PLI-driven manufacturing expansion creates a substantial domestic demand base for electronics components and semiconductors within India. This domestic demand is a foundational market signal for the India Semiconductor Mission's chip manufacturing investments: Micron Sanand, Tata Dholera, CG Power, and Kaynes Semicon all target the Indian electronics manufacturing market created by PLI beneficiaries as their primary domestic customer. The virtuous cycle: PLI drives electronics assembly in India → creates domestic demand for semiconductor packaging and chips → supports ISM semiconductor facility investments → reduces import dependence → strengthens supply chain resilience for Indian electronics manufacturers.

PLI FOR IT HARDWARE (LAPTOPS, TABLETS, SERVERS): A separate PLI scheme for IT hardware was approved in 2021 with ₹7,325 crore outlay, targeting manufacturers of laptops, tablets, servers, and networking equipment. Approved manufacturers include HP India, Lenovo India, Dell India, and Acer India. This scheme creates domestic manufacturing anchor points for the PC and server market — the product categories where semiconductor supply chain disruption (chip shortage) directly translates to inventory shortfalls on Flipkart's electronics platform.

MITIGATION RELEVANCE — ALTERNATE PROCUREMENT ROUTES: When the Mitigation Recommendation Agent evaluates alternate sourcing in response to China-origin or Taiwan-origin electronics supply chain disruption, PLI-registered manufacturers in India should be prioritized as alternate procurement sources for finished goods. This is because: (1) PLI manufacturers have committed to maintaining Indian production at scale; (2) They operate under government oversight with production accountability; (3) Their supply chains are incentivized to move toward domestic component sourcing (ISM chips, domestic PCBs, domestic display manufacturing) over PLI eligibility periods; (4) India-origin electronics products face lower geopolitical and export control risk for Flipkart's domestic customer base than equivalent China-assembled products.
