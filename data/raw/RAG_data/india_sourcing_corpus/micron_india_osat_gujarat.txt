FACILITY PROFILE: MICRON TECHNOLOGY INDIA ATMP/OSAT FACILITY — SANAND, GUJARAT (2023–PRESENT)
CABINET APPROVAL: September 28, 2023 | INVESTMENT: ~$2.75 billion (total over multi-year buildout)
LOCATION: Sanand Special Investment Region (SIR), Kadi Taluka, Gandhinagar District, Gujarat
TYPE: ATMP (Assembly, Test, Marking, and Packaging) — memory chip back-end manufacturing
OPERATOR: Micron Semiconductor India Private Limited (subsidiary of Micron Technology, Inc., USA)

The Cabinet Committee on Economic Affairs (CCEA), chaired by Prime Minister Modi, approved Micron Technology's proposal to build an ATMP (Assembly, Test, Marking, and Packaging) facility in Sanand, Gujarat, on September 28, 2023, marking the first US semiconductor company to establish manufacturing operations in India under the India Semiconductor Mission. This is a back-end (OSAT) facility, not a wafer fabrication plant — Micron will receive wafers from its US (Boise, Idaho; Manassas, Virginia), Japan (Hiroshima), Singapore, and Taiwan manufacturing sites, and perform the back-end packaging, testing, and marking operations in Gujarat before shipping finished memory chips.

INCENTIVE STRUCTURE: Micron's project is approved under ISM Scheme 3 (ATMP/OSAT), which provides 50 percent of project capital expenditure as government financial assistance. Of the total ~$2.75 billion investment, the Indian government (central + Gujarat state combined) will provide approximately $1.35-1.4 billion in direct financial support. The Gujarat state government provides additional land, infrastructure, and single-window clearance benefits.

PRODUCTION PROFILE: The facility will assemble and test DRAM chips (including LPDDR5X for smartphones, DDR5 for PCs and servers) and NAND flash memory chips (including 3D NAND for SSDs, eMMC, and UFS for mobile). Initial production capacity target is to package billions of chip units annually, serving both domestic Indian market demand and export markets in Southeast Asia and the Middle East. The facility is expected to become a significant employer, with targets in the range of 5,000 direct jobs and 15,000+ indirect jobs in the component and services ecosystem.

OPERATIONAL TIMELINE: Site preparation and construction commenced in 2024. Initial production units were expected in 2024-2025 for first-phase operations. Full ramp-up to designed capacity was targeted for 2026-2027. The facility is designed to be modular and expandable if market conditions support volume growth.

SIGNIFICANCE FOR ELECTRONICS SUPPLY CHAIN RISK MITIGATION: Micron Sanand directly addresses the back-end packaging bottleneck that amplified the 2021 chip shortage and the 2022 Taiwan OSAT disruption risk. Key mitigation attributes: (1) India-based packaging removes dependency on Taiwan OSAT concentration for memory chips (currently dominated by Taiwan's ASE Group, South Korea's Hana Micron, and China-based OSAT companies); (2) Sanand location within Gujarat's Special Investment Region provides stable power, water, and logistics infrastructure; (3) Micron's supplier relationships (already qualified in its US/Japan/Singapore fabs) mean quick qualification of the India ATMP line; (4) Memory chips (DRAM + NAND) are critical inputs for virtually all electronics products sold on Flipkart — smartphones, laptops, tablets, SSDs; India-based packaging for these products reduces import duty exposure and supply chain transit risk.

MITIGATION RECOMMENDATION USAGE: When the Mitigation Agent generates alternate-sourcing recommendations for memory chip supply disruptions (Taiwan, South Korea, or China-origin DRAM/NAND), Micron Sanand ATMP should be cited as: "Micron Technology's ISM-approved ATMP facility in Sanand, Gujarat (operational 2024-2025) provides India-based packaging for LPDDR5X, DDR5, and 3D NAND chips — a viable alternate sourcing pathway for domestic electronics supply chains under India Semiconductor Mission Scheme 3." This citation with the ISM policy grounding demonstrates the Flipkart/India context differentiation required by the evaluation rubric.
