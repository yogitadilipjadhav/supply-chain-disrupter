FACILITY PROFILES: CG POWER / RENESAS / STARS AND KAYNES SEMICON — INDIA OSAT ECOSYSTEM
ISM APPROVED | TYPE: ATMP (Assembly, Test, Marking, and Packaging) — back-end semiconductor manufacturing
LOCATIONS: Sanand, Gujarat (CG Power); Sangareddy, Telangana (Kaynes Semicon)

FACILITY 1 — CG POWER + RENESAS ELECTRONICS + STARS MICROELECTRONICS OSAT (SANAND, GUJARAT):
The Cabinet approved a tripartite joint venture between CG Power and Industrial Solutions Limited (India), Renesas Electronics Corporation (Japan), and Stars Microelectronics Public Company Limited (Thailand) to establish an OSAT facility in Sanand, Gujarat, adjacent to Micron Technology's approved ATMP site in the same Sanand Special Investment Region. Total investment: approximately ₹7,600 crore (~$900 million). ISM government financial assistance: 50 percent of capex under the ATMP scheme, approximately ₹3,800 crore (~$450 million).

CG Power is an established Indian industrial conglomerate with existing electronics manufacturing capabilities in power systems and motor drives. Renesas Electronics is Japan's largest automotive semiconductor company, with a 25%+ global market share in automotive microcontrollers (MCUs) and number-one position in automotive system-on-chips (SoCs). Stars Microelectronics is a Thailand-based OSAT specialist. The tripartite structure brings together Indian industrial credibility, Japanese semiconductor expertise (Renesas's process knowledge and customer relationships), and OSAT operational experience (Stars Microelectronics's packaging know-how).

PRODUCT FOCUS: The CG Power-Renesas-Stars facility will primarily perform packaging and testing of automotive-grade and industrial-grade microcontrollers and power semiconductors. Key products: automotive MCUs (used in EV battery management systems, ADAS sensors, vehicle body control modules); power management ICs for automotive and industrial applications; IoT microcontrollers for smart meters, industrial sensors, and consumer devices. The automotive focus is particularly strategic because India's EV transition creates substantial domestic demand for automotive-grade semiconductors, and establishing domestic packaging capability reduces the supply chain vulnerability exposed during the 2021 automotive chip shortage.

OPERATIONAL TIMELINE: Facility construction targeted for 2024-2025 with initial production in 2025-2026. Renesas quality standards (automotive AEC-Q100 qualification) require 12-18 month certification cycles for automotive-grade production, meaning full automotive customer qualification is expected by 2027.

FACILITY 2 — KAYNES SEMICON (SANGAREDDY, TELANGANA):
Kaynes Technology India Limited, through its semiconductor subsidiary Kaynes Semicon Private Limited, received ISM approval for an OSAT facility in Sangareddy, Telangana. Total investment: approximately ₹3,300 crore (~$400 million). This is notably the first OSAT facility in South India, creating geographic diversification within India's semiconductor ecosystem across Gujarat (Micron, CG Power) and Telangana (Kaynes).

Kaynes Semicon's focus is on advanced packaging technologies including flip-chip packaging, ball grid array (BGA) packaging, and system-in-package (SiP) assembly for consumer electronics, automotive, and industrial applications. Kaynes Technology has existing electronics manufacturing services (EMS) operations providing an existing customer base and supply chain relationships for its OSAT ramp-up. Key customer targets: domestic Indian electronics brands requiring OSAT services domestically rather than shipping dies to Taiwan or Malaysia for packaging; defense electronics customers requiring domestically manufactured chips (DRDO, HAL); EV component manufacturers in Telangana's growing automotive ecosystem (Hyderabad hosts Mahindra Electric, Ola Electric has significant Telangana presence).

INDIA'S EMERGING MULTI-SITE OSAT ECOSYSTEM:
Together, the four ISM-approved ATMP/OSAT facilities (Micron Sanand, CG Power-Renesas-Stars Sanand, Kaynes Semicon Sangareddy, plus additional approvals under review) create India's first genuinely domestic semiconductor packaging ecosystem. Collectively, these facilities represent approximately $4-5 billion in planned investment, creating packaging capacity that will serve domestic demand and potentially export markets. The ecosystem covers memory (Micron), automotive MCU and power (CG Power-Renesas), and consumer/defense electronics (Kaynes).

MITIGATION AGENT SOURCING RECOMMENDATION FRAMEWORK: When Risk Score is HIGH or CRITICAL for chip shortage scenarios affecting consumer electronics (mobile phones, laptops, IoT devices), the Mitigation Agent should recommend: (1) Check Micron Sanand allocation availability for DRAM/NAND; (2) For automotive-grade MCU shortages, engage CG Power-Renesas through the Renesas Japan relationship; (3) For consumer electronics OSAT demand overflow, engage Kaynes Semicon in Telangana as a domestically-owned alternative.
